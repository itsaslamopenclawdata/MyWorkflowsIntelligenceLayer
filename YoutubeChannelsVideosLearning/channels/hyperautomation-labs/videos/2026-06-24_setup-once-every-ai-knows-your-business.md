# Set This Up Once & Every AI Knows Your Business Forever (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini)

## TL;DR

Stop re-explaining yourself in every AI chat. Build a "portable AI brain" — a small set of plain-text files organized in three layers (who you are, what you're working on, what works) — and point any AI at it. The same files power Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini identically. Let an AI (Claude Code / Cowork) scaffold the folders and interview you to fill them in (~5 minutes), then maintain a weekly wins log (~2 min/week). The AI becomes the disposable part; your context becomes the asset that compounds.

## Key Insights

- **The real problem isn't prompts or tools — it's portable context.** Memory that lives inside one AI app is fragile and gets abandoned the moment a better tool arrives. Fix context ownership, and tool choice becomes a commodity.
- **The "portable AI brain" is a file system, not an app.** Plain Markdown files (about-you, about-business, voice, offers, project briefs, references, examples, wins log) — readable by Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, and any future model. No vendor lock-in.
- **Three-layer structure:**
  1. **Who you are** (4 files): about-you, about-business, voice, offers. Barely changes once written.
  2. **What you're working on** (one folder per project): brief, optional project-specific voice file, references folder, examples folder (3 best-work samples is the magic number).
  3. **Wins log** — the compound-interest layer. One line per win; this is what most people skip and it's what makes the system actually get smarter over time.
- **The voice file is the highest-leverage file in the whole system.** Generic AI output vs. voice-attached output on the same prompt is night-and-day. Build it by handing the AI 3–5 real pieces of your own writing (emails, newsletters, posts) and having it describe your voice back to you.
- **Examples > instructions.** A folder of 3 best-work samples teaches the AI faster than any prompt.
- **Don't build the brain by hand — let the AI build it.** In Claude Code or Claude Cowork, describe the structure, ask it to scaffold the folders and files, then have it interview you one question at a time. ~5 minutes total, no blank-page paralysis.
- **The same brain, three AIs, identical results.** The video demonstrates the exact same files attached to Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini with the identical prompt ("suggest three newsletter topics in my voice") and gets on-brand output from all three. Proves the brain is the asset, the model is the disposable layer.
- **Maintenance is ~2 minutes/week.** Add one line to the wins log whenever something works (a subject line that crushed, a phrasing a client loved). Update one file when the business actually changes. No app to babysit.
- **Compounding payoff:** after a year of weekly wins-log entries, your AI understands your business better than a new hire would after 6 months — because it's been fed a continuous stream of validated feedback, not a one-time onboarding doc.
