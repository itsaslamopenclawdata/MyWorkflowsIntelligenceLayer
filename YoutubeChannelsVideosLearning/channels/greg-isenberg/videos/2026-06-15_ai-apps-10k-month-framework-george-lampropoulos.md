# AI Apps Are Making $10K/Month. Here's Exactly How.

## TL;DR
George Lampropoulos (19, ex-TJ Max cashier) breaks down the framework behind his AI-built mobile apps (Wrestle AI: 100K downloads + ~$200K revenue; two stealth apps on track for $10–15K/mo) working 3–4 hours/week each. The playbook: pick a niche problem you're personally passionate about, build with vibe coding tools in ~14 days of iterating (not one-shotting), reverse-engineer your ICP's For You Page into a scroll-and-DM outreach machine, pay a VA to scale it, run paid ads with high-agency creators remaking competitor creatives one-to-one, and optimize for ARPU ≥ $2 + retention via "sticky" adjacent features. The single highest-leverage insight: **a bad idea with great distribution still loses** — the 1.8M-view "AI rizz" app generated 5 weekly subs while a 1M-view niche app generated $17K.

## Key Insights
- $10K/month is just $333/day — one or two things done right gets you there (0:55)
- Three legs of a successful app: a good idea, a product that matches it, distribution (2:35)
- "Solve your own problems and create something you're passionate about" — passion survives 10-hour sessions and is visible to influencers on calls (2:48)
- AI unlocked two new things: AI-first experiences better than existing ones (e.g., CalAI: photo of food → calories) AND democratized niche-app creation without capital (5:25)
- Reverse-engineer app ideas: scroll TikTok/IG for 15 min, ask "Who watches this? What problem do they have? How could an influencer promote this seamlessly in the first 30 sec without it feeling like an ad?" (6:30)
- The Green app lesson: 1.8M views → $35. Wrestle AI: 1M views → $17K. Distribution amplifies the idea; a bad mission won't convert (8:25)
- Convince influencers with 50/50 revenue split when you have no capital + zero leverage; pitch your existing conversion rate as proof (11:15)
- The "mousetrap" test: your product should bring users back daily and create so much value they can't leave — that's what makes influencers say yes to long-term partnerships (11:50)
- Boot a niche Instagram brand account showcasing past collabs BEFORE influencer outreach — brand authority + "everyone else works for these rates" framing unlocks $2 CPM deals vs. zero rates (24:30)
- Tailor your FYP to be your ICP — scroll, comment, share as them — then DM from the resulting lead-generating feed (27:55)
- Replace yourself ASAP: spend ~$800 of $2K starting budget on a VA who runs your scroll-outreach SOP on Fiverr/Upwork (28:55)
- Hop on a call the moment an influencer responds — face-to-face is harder to overcharge than DMs; get them on the phone before they anchor on $10K/video rates (30:50)
- Influencer marketing = 90% sales: sell the long-term partnership and the genuine product, not the one-off check (10:55)
- The Meta Ads Library reverse-engineering trick: search Strava's paid ads, filter by high impressions, send the highest-performing video to a high-agency creator and ask them to remake it one-to-one with your app (34:50)
- Paid ads don't need to be polished — "Go download the app" videos from real creators convert (36:00)
- After 100 downloads/day, audit conversion rate + ARPU; aim for $2 ARPU in month one (37:25)
- Retention fix: Wrestle AI added a calorie tracker (adjacent, non-viral feature for wrestlers managing weight) and **churn cut in half** — viral feature for acquisition, useful feature for retention (37:50)
- Vibe coding in Swift vs. React Native is "night and day" — Swift+Opus 4.8 with 14 days of manual iteration beats AI-slop stigma (40:30)
