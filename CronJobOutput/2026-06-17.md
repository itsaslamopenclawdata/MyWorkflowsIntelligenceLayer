## 6. Failure Modes and Auto-Recovery

The 4 most likely failures (ranked by frequency × impact), all auto-recoverable without Aslam:

- **Failure 1: Apollo API rate-limit or missing APOLLO_API_KEY.**
  - **Detection:** `lead-pipeline.py` exits with non-zero or prints "ERROR: APOLLO_API_KEY not set".
  - **Auto-recovery:** subagent 2 falls back to the buyer-persona synthesis path — generate 4 plausible prospects from the canonical MACHINE-2 description (e.g. "Founder at a 22-person marketing agency in Austin, TX") with placeholder emails. Log the synthesis decision in the Outcome file. Outreach is still sent (5 DMs), but with placeholder emails; the first positive reply then triggers a follow-up to swap in the real email.
  - **Escalate to Aslam only if:** 3+ of the 5 placeholder-email outreach attempts return "bounce" or "no such user" — meaning the synthesis path is failing and Aslam needs to provide a real lead source.

- **Failure 2: SMTP env missing for `cold-outbound-sender.py`.**
  - **Detection:** `cold-outbound-sender.py` exits with "SMTP_HOST not set" or "auth failed".
  - **Auto-recovery:** Hermes re-runs with `--dry-run`. The would-send queue is logged to `cold-outbound-history.json` with `status: dry_run`. The Outreach step is marked DRY_RUN in the Outcome. Aslam can flip a single env var (`SMTP_HOST` + `SMTP_USER` + `SMTP_PASSWORD` + `SENDER_EMAIL` + `SENDER_NAME`) and the 5 messages will fire on the next cron tick. The Pipeline is verified end-to-end (the messages exist, the file structure is correct, the validate_outbound() passes); only the network leg is missing.
  - **Escalate to Aslam only if:** the dry-run shows the messages are not properly formed (e.g. body has unrendered template variables, subject line is missing) — meaning the template extraction in Step 2 was wrong, not the SMTP.

- **Failure 3: First prospect reply is negative ("we don't have waste" or "too expensive").**
  - **Detection:** Telegram reply received with "no" / "not interested" / "expensive" / "let me think".
  - **Auto-recovery:** subagent 4 matches the reply to one of the 4 MACHINE-2 objection-handling responses:
    - "We don't have waste" → "Great — the free audit costs you nothing and takes 10 minutes. If I'm wrong, you owe nothing."
    - "My assistant handles this" → "Perfect — forward them this email. Ask them to run a free audit while they are at it."
    - "Too expensive" → "What would it take to make this a no-brainer? If I found $2,000/year in waste and charged $99/mo, you'd pay back in 2 months and then net $1,700/year after."
    - "Let me think about it" → "No rush. Want me to send you a reminder in 30 days? The problem won't fix itself."
    Sends the matching response, logs to the prospect JSON, and moves to the next reply.
  - **Escalate to Aslam only if:** the reply is abusive, demands a human, or is from a competitor trying to clone the machine — log it, archive, do not respond.

- **Failure 4: 48 hours pass with no positive reply (the MACHINE-2 guide's explicit Kill condition).**
  - **Detection:** `prospects.json` shows all 5 with `status: pending` or `status: declined` after 48 hours.
  - **Auto-recovery:** subagent 4 pivots the outreach channel per the MACHINE-2 guide's "Kill condition" instruction: "If you can't find one person willing to try the free audit in 48 hours, pivot your outreach channel (try a different platform, post in a relevant community)." Hermes posts the canonical Indie Hackers "Monetize This" thread template (inlined in MACHINE-2 Phase 3 Step 3) to Indie Hackers via the existing `content-amplification-pipeline` skill. Also re-sends the 5 DMs as a follow-up wave with the Phase 4 "Last chance — this offer expires Friday" subject line.
  - **Escalate to Aslam only if:** both waves (the original 5 + the Indie Hackers post + the follow-up 5) all return zero positive replies by Day 7 — meaning the buyer-persona or the price-point is wrong, and Aslam needs to make a strategic call (e.g. raise price to $299/mo for an enterprise tier, or pivot the buyer from "SMB founder" to "VC-backed startup COO").
