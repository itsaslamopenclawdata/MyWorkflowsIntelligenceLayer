# Your AI Reads Everything But Connects Nothing — GraphRAG Explained

**Channel:** Hyperautomation Labs  **Published:** 2026-06-23  **Duration:** 13:04  **Watch:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5LBj7XSaxSI

## TL;DR
Vector RAG fails on three question classes: multi-fact stitching, multi-hop reasoning, and global ("what are the main themes?") queries — because top-K similarity only ever sees isolated chunks. GraphRAG (Microsoft Research) replaces similarity search with a knowledge graph built by LLM-extracted entities and relationships, clustered by Leiden community detection, with per-community summaries pre-written. Local search handles entity lookups; global search uses map-reduce over the community summaries. Microsoft benchmarks show GraphRAG wins 72–83% on comprehensiveness and 62–82% on diversity, using 97%+ fewer tokens than stuffing source text — at higher indexing cost. Use LazyGraphRAG when budget is tight (≈0.1% of full GraphRAG indexing cost).

## Key Insights
- Vector RAG's quiet assumption — every answer lives in one nearby chunk — is exactly where it breaks. (0:45)
- Three failure modes: (1) connecting-the-dots across documents, (2) multi-hop reasoning where bridge facts don't look similar to the question, (3) global summarization questions that require reasoning over the whole corpus. (1:52)
- GraphRAG indexing pipeline: chunk → LLM extracts entities + relationships per chunk → merge duplicates across chunks → Leiden community detection → LLM writes a hierarchical summary for every community at every level. (4:08)
- Two query modes: local search (zoom into one entity + neighbors + source text) and global search (map-reduce across all community summaries, ranked by importance). (6:16)
- Microsoft's win rates — GraphRAG preferred 72–83% on comprehensiveness and 62–82% on diversity vs. vector RAG. Global-search token cost is >97% lower than stuffing full source text into context. (7:25)
- LazyGraphRAG skips heavy up-front summarization and reports indexing cost of ~0.1% of full GraphRAG — basically the same price as plain vector RAG while keeping most of the quality. (10:08)
- Vector RAG still wins on directness for narrow factual lookups — GraphRAG earns its keep on the connected questions. (8:19)
- Best-fit domains: fraud and financial investigation (a scheme invisible in one transaction but obvious across accounts), enterprise knowledge bases (wikis+tickets+chat), legal (statutes→contracts→case-law chains), medical research (patients↔studies↔findings). (8:40)
