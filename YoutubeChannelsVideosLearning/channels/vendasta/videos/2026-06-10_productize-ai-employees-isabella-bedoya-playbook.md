## Action Items
- [ ] **Do the productization audit:** For your current service offering, list every step that requires a human to do work for that specific client. If a step is human-centric, it caps your multiple. The productizable steps are the ones that scale.
- [ ] **Pick a vertical and own it.** Isabella's solar example: first client takes 10x effort, clients 2-10 are copy-paste. Don't try to serve 10 verticals at once.
- [ ] **Apply the "minimum viable agent" pattern for receptionist rollouts:** Start by automating the top 3-5 questions, NOT the full service catalog. Document everything. Build support materials. Climb the pricing ladder only after support cost is real.
- [ ] **For your first $100K:** cap client count to protect delivery quality. Document case studies. Get video testimonials. THEN scale.
- [ ] **Add a "speed-to-lead" agent as an upsell** to any existing ad-management or lead-gen client. The math (faster contact → higher conversion) sells itself.
- [ ] **Weekly prompt-iteration ritual:** Treat your AI agent like a human collaborator. Listen to a few of its calls, find where it failed, rewrite the prompt, retest. The agents that win are the ones whose owners iterate weekly.
- [ ] **Defend against shiny-object syndrome:** Pre-commit to a 90-day focus window. No new partnerships, no new verticals, no new platforms until the current one is humming.
