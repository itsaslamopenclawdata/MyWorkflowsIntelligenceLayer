## TL;DR

A viral screenshot claims you can fire your whole team and run a company on a $400/month stack of AI agents. The video actually does the hiring math role by role and concludes the stack is real leverage but **does not replace a five-person team** — it gives one very busy human roughly 1.5–2 roles of leverage, and the $400 is a floor not a ceiling. The four "breaks" where the pitch fails are: (1) the **Supervisor Tax** — you don't remove work, you become the manager (Salesforce still employs its engineers while spending ~$300M on Anthropic in 2026, mostly to supervise coding agents); (2) **Reliability** — Devin completed 3/20 real tasks in an independent test, and public benchmark scores for fully autonomous software issue resolution sit in the low teens; (3) **Accountability** — you can't fire, PIP, or depose an agent, so liability collapses onto the one human; (4) the **Cost Ceiling** — Anthropic's own estimate is $150–$250/month per heavy coding user, with top tier past $600. Net: replacement is a fantasy, leverage is the strategy. Winners are solo founders who use agents to skip the next three hires and pour time into judgment, taste, relationships, and risk.
